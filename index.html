Artist representations Over Time in MoMA

Frequency of recorded male and female paintings across years

This chart shows gender representation in MoMA’s collection over time. Male artists dominate from the 19th century onward, while female representation grows gradually in the late 20th century. Use the legend to isolate each group and explore their historical peaks.

Male Artist Dominance: Historical Peaks

  • 1930–1948 — Interwar and post–World War II institutional consolidation.
  • 1937 — Expansion of modernist narratives during global political tension.
  • 1945 — Post-war canon formation centered on male modernists.
  • 1965 — Highest peak (over 2,000 artworks), coinciding with MoMA’s expansion and canon solidification.
  • 1991 — Market-driven collecting during late modernism.
  • 2003 — Globalized contemporary art still dominated by male artists.

Female Artist Visibility: Key Moments

  • 1899 — Early but marginal inclusion of women artists.
  • 1931 — Interwar visibility, still institutionally limited.
  • 1977–1979 — Feminist art movements and institutional critique.
  • 1999 — Highest peak (≈570 artworks), reflecting curatorial reassessment and feminist art history.
